What is actually delivered?

A front-end diagnostic path that helps you decide whether the idea deserves more work. It is for triage, not a finished business plan.

Strategy Pack

Documents and business planning assets: offer, customer, strategy, revenue model, GTM, operations, and related artifacts. No live app deployment.

Foundation Build

Generated code and delivery assets: app surface, API/backend files, deployment scaffolding, tests, seed data, docs, and handoff materials.

Server-side provisioning attempts run only when the plan, archetype, credentials, and vendor APIs allow. Failed or missing vendor steps become receipts and manual actions.

Business Factory API

Production admin endpoint that generates launch manifests and queues jobs. A queued job is not the same as a completed vendor setup.

Delivery Receipts

Tests, manifests, maturity pages, and docs show what was generated, what was attempted, what passed, and what still requires operator action.

What this does not promise

Not guaranteed revenue

Revenue still depends on market demand, traffic, buyer trust, offer quality, follow-up, and execution.

Not hidden vendor magic

Domains, email, Stripe, CRM, analytics, and automations depend on configured accounts, credentials, vendor approval, and tier rules.

Plain boundary: if a page says “build,” it means generated files, deployment scaffolding, tests, and handoff assets. If a third-party account is required, the receipt will show whether it ran, failed, or needs manual action.
