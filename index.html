FAQ
Common questions

What exactly gets provisioned?

Live automatically: Website deployed to Vercel, GitHub repo with 30+ files, Stripe products and webhooks, 20i hosting with DNS/email/SPF/DKIM, n8n automation workflows, PostHog analytics project, Neon database.

Created as drafts (need one-click activation): Acumbamail email campaigns, SuiteDash CRM pipeline and portal, SMS templates.

Generated to repo (need template support): SEO blog posts (markdown), legal document templates, video scripts, design briefs.

Your OPERATIONS.md documents exactly what's live, what needs activation, and step-by-step instructions for everything.

Are these real accounts or just templates?

Real API calls to real platforms. Stripe products and webhooks are created live. n8n automation workflows are deployed. Email marketing lists and draft campaigns are created. DNS records and email mailboxes are configured. Some services (email sequences, CRM pipelines, SMS campaigns) are set up as drafts or templates that require manual activation — your OPERATIONS.md documents exactly what's live and what needs one-click activation.

Do I own everything?

You own all generated code and documents. Code is pushed to a GitHub repo (under the pinohu org by default, or your own org if configured). Domain registration is separate — Dynasty configures DNS and hosting, but you purchase the domain. Third-party service accounts (Stripe products, n8n workflows, email lists) are provisioned under Dynasty's platform credentials. You receive full access credentials in CREDENTIALS.md and an OPERATIONS.md owner's manual. For full ownership, migrate services to your own accounts using the credentials and instructions provided.

What if a module fails?

No module failure blocks the build. Every module has graceful fallbacks — if an API is unreachable or a key is missing, you get manual setup instructions in your OPERATIONS.md. The build completes with whatever succeeded.

How is this different from Shopify or Wix?

Those build websites. Dynasty provisions an entire operating business — CRM, phone, email marketing, billing, automation, analytics, legal docs, SEO content — all connected and working together from minute one. Plus you own the code.

What deployment targets are supported?

Next.js on Vercel (SaaS, dashboards, AI platforms), WordPress on 20i (authority sites, local businesses), Brilliant Directories (membership/directory sites), static HTML on Vercel (landing pages), and SuiteDash (client portal businesses).

Are there ongoing costs after the build?

The build fee is one-time. However, provisioned services may have their own costs: Stripe charges transaction fees, domain registration is annual, 20i hosting has monthly fees, and some tools have usage-based pricing. Your OPERATIONS.md documents all services and their pricing. Vercel and Neon have generous free tiers that cover most early-stage usage.

Are the legal documents production-ready?

Legal document templates (Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Service Agreement) are AI-generated starting points customized to your business. They are not a substitute for professional legal advice. We strongly recommend having an attorney review them before publishing.
