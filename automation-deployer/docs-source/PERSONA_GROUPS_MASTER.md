### PERSONA 1: "Solo Steve" — The Solo Service Provider

**Demographics:** Age 28-45. Runs a one-person service business (cleaning, handyman, consulting, bookkeeping, photography). Revenue $40K-$150K. Tech comfort: basic (phone-heavy, minimal desktop).

**Psychographics:** Wears every hat. Proud of being independent but quietly drowning. Does client work during the day, admin at night. Misses follow-ups because there's no system. Spouse is frustrated by the hours. Knows there has to be a better way but terrified of complex software. Has tried one or two tools and abandoned them.

**Goals:** Stop working 70-hour weeks. Never miss a lead again. Look professional. Eventually hire someone but needs systems first.

**Frustrations:** Leads slip through cracks. Invoices go out late. No time to market. Every tool demo assumes you have a team. Can't justify $300/mo for software when revenue fluctuates.

**Buying behavior:** Cheapest entry point. Will pay $19-49/mo for something that saves time THIS WEEK. Buys on mobile. Cancels fast if onboarding takes >15 minutes. Word-of-mouth driven.

**Key quote:** *"I didn't start a business to do paperwork all night."*

**Sells to:** Packages 1.1, 1.3, 1.5 | Hero Workflows: Missed Call Text-Back, Appointment Reminders

### PERSONA 2: "Manager Maria" — The Small Team Leader

**Demographics:** Age 32-50. Manages a service business with 3-15 employees. Revenue $250K-$2M. Roles: operations manager, office manager, or owner who transitioned from doing work to managing. Tech comfort: moderate.

**Psychographics:** Constantly context-switching. Knows the business runs on tribal knowledge in people's heads. Has experienced a key employee leaving and taking all process knowledge. Wants to systematize but keeps getting pulled into fires.

**Goals:** Build processes that don't depend on any single person. Reduce client complaints from dropped balls. Get visibility into what her team does. Stop being the bottleneck.

**Frustrations:** Team members forget steps. Onboarding differs every time. No way to know if follow-ups happen. Reports require pulling data from 5 places. Staff turnover means retraining from scratch.

**Buying behavior:** Pays $99-299/mo for proven solutions. Needs ROI in 30 days. Influenced by case studies from similar-sized businesses. Will champion purchase internally but needs to justify to owner.

**Key quote:** *"Every time someone quits, we lose six months of institutional knowledge."*

**Sells to:** Packages 1.1, 1.2, 1.6, 2.1 | Hero Workflows: Lead Nurture, New Client Onboarding

### PERSONA 3: "Owner Omar" — The Growth-Stage Business Owner

**Demographics:** Age 35-55. Owns service business with 15-50 employees. Revenue $2M-$10M. Has office manager and department leads but still makes most strategic decisions. Tech comfort: moderate to high.

**Psychographics:** Past survival stage, focused on scaling. Realizes what got him here won't get him there. Business grows linearly with headcount and wants to break that. Reads business books, attends masterminds. Knows automation is the answer but overwhelmed by options.

**Goals:** Scale revenue without proportionally scaling headcount. Systematize sales so it doesn't depend on him. Real-time visibility into business health. Build a business that runs without him.

**Frustrations:** Revenue grows but margins stay flat because he keeps hiring. Sales pipeline is a black box. Can't compare marketing channels. Spends weekends doing thinking work.

**Buying behavior:** Invests $299-999/mo. Buys annual plans. Wants a strategic partner, not just a tool. Influenced by peer recommendations at events. Pays for done-for-you setup.

**Key quote:** *"I've got 40 employees and still feel like a one-man show."*

**Sells to:** Packages 1.1, 1.4, 1.6, 1.7, 7.1 | Hero Workflows: Full Stack Bundle

### PERSONA 7: "Field Service Fred" — The Field-Based Business Owner

**Demographics:** Age 30-55. Runs a service business where technicians go to client sites (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pest control, landscaping, appliance repair). Revenue $500K-$5M. 5-30 field technicians. Tech comfort: moderate.

**Goals:** Real-time visibility into technician locations and job status. Eliminate paper. Automate dispatch and scheduling. Get paid faster with on-site invoicing. Reduce "truck rolls" (sending wrong tech or wrong parts).

**Frustrations:** Can't answer "where's my technician?" calls. Technicians complete jobs but forget to close them in the system. Double-booking happens weekly. Parts inventory is wrong, causing return trips. Cash flow suffers from slow invoicing.

**Buying behavior:** Pays $149-399/mo. Needs mobile-first solution. Technicians must adopt it (if they won't use it, nothing works). Needs offline capability. Integration with QuickBooks is non-negotiable.

**Key quote:** *"I have 15 trucks on the road and no idea where any of them are right now."*

**Sells to:** Packages 1.1, 1.3, 1.5, 6.1 | HVAC/Plumbing vertical (PAID Filter 4/4)

## USAGE NOTES

1. **For landing pages:** Lead with the persona's Key Quote as the headline. Address their top 2 frustrations in the subheadline. Show before/after proof matching their revenue range.

2. **For email sequences:** Use the buying behavior section to set pricing anchors and urgency triggers. Match send frequency to their tech comfort level.

3. **For ads:** Target based on demographics. Use frustration language in ad copy. Segment campaigns by persona.

4. **For sales calls:** Open with their frustration. Present the Hero Workflow that maps to their persona. Price using their buying behavior range.

5. **For content:** Write articles that speak to 2-3 personas simultaneously. Use their language, not technical jargon.
