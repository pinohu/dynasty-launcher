# Blue Ocean Hungry Market Framework

Niche selection rubric for any new directory, vertical SaaS, or authority
site. A niche must clear this gate before provisioning begins.

## The four tests

### 1. Hungry market

The audience is already spending money trying to solve the problem. Signals:

- Active paid ads on Google / Meta / YouTube targeting the pain.
- Thriving paid communities, courses, or newsletters in the space.
- Existing directories, BD sites, or SaaS tools charging for it — even
  poorly-built ones are a positive signal.
- Recurring complaints in Reddit / Facebook groups about "nothing works"
  or "I'd pay for X."

No hunger → no niche. Education markets and awareness markets are
disqualified; Dynasty does not create demand.

### 2. Blue ocean within the hungry market

The hungry market is crowded overall, but a specific vertical, geography,
regulation, or persona inside it is underserved. Signals:

- Top Google result for the specific phrasing is a forum thread, not a
  dedicated property.
- No directory dominates the niche (or the dominant one is > 5 years old
  and visibly stale).
- A specific state, industry, or certification creates a moat the big
  horizontals haven't bothered with.

"PA notary services" is blue ocean. "Notary services" is not.

### 3. Authority fit

Dynasty can credibly publish in this niche. Credible = one of:

- Direct professional credential (PA notary, MLO, real estate, engineering
  professor).
- Adjacent operating business (CROP services → surplus funds, probate,
  real-estate-adjacent verticals).
- Academic/research background that maps (HCI, human factors, UX research
  → SaaS, fintech, enterprise tooling).

A niche where we have to pretend expertise fails this test. Partner or pass.

### 4. Entity-and-compliance viability

- No license we don't have.
- No regulated activity we can't indirectly provide (legal advice,
  medical diagnosis, financial fiduciary duty).
- LLC routing is clear per the entity routing principle.

A niche that would require a new license, a new LLC filing, or novel
compliance work requires escalation before provisioning begins.

## Scoring

Each test is pass/fail. All four must pass. A niche that fails any test
is not "weaker" — it is disqualified. The Launcher generates hundreds of
possible niches per week; there is no reason to pursue a borderline one.

## Anti-niches (never provision)

- Anything targeting minors or youth-specific markets.
- Anything requiring HIPAA-covered data handling unless explicitly scoped
  and staffed.
- Anything targeting vulnerable populations (addiction recovery, crisis
  housing, immigration detention) — these need human-in-the-loop operation
  Dynasty does not currently staff.
- Anything requiring a fiduciary relationship (investment advice,
  financial planning) — MLO license covers origination only.

## Priority tiers within passing niches

1. **Tier 1**: Niche feeds an existing Dynasty revenue engine (PA CROP,
   ManifestIQ) or directly adjacent to one.
2. **Tier 2**: Niche inside an established PA or national vertical where
   authority site + directory + SuiteDash stack applies cleanly.
3. **Tier 3**: Novel niche that passes all four tests but has no existing
   Dynasty tie — ship only after one T1/T2 slot is filled.
