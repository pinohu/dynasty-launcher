# UX Copy Standard - Your Deputy

Use this file when writing customer-facing offer, provisioning, checkout, and launched-deliverable copy.

## The Promise Formula

Every paid offer must be written as:

`You provide [inputs]. Your Deputy creates [outputs]. You receive [access point]. Vendor-owned services require [credentials/boundary].`

Example:

`You provide business details, launch copy, and customer-owned vendor credentials. Your Deputy creates the launch page, lead form, workflow templates, CRM seed data, runtime files, activation receipt, and public launch URL. Twilio, Stripe, Resend, calendar, domain, and CRM actions require valid customer-owned credentials.`

## Forbidden Phrases Without Proof

Do not use these phrases unless the same page names the backend object, URL, workflow, or receipt that proves it:

- fully autonomous
- business factory
- one-click company
- money before product
- no manual steps
- done-for-you everything
- launched business
- autopilot revenue

## Required Page Sections

Every provisioning page must include:

- What will be created.
- What credentials are required.
- What happens after submission.
- What the customer receives.
- What may require a vendor action or valid account.

Every launched deliverable page must include:

- Customer business name.
- Offer name.
- Active lead capture form.
- Activated modules.
- Provisioned components.
- Clear live status.

Every sales page must include:

- Price.
- Outcome.
- Included artifacts.
- Not included / requires credentials.
- Link to a full live example.

## Error Copy

Errors should name the next action:

- Bad: `Provisioning failed.`
- Good: `Provisioning stopped because the Stripe secret key was missing. Add the customer-owned Stripe key and submit again.`

## Success Copy

Success should name the result:

- Bad: `Done.`
- Good: `Your launch is created. Open the live deliverable URL and test lead capture.`
