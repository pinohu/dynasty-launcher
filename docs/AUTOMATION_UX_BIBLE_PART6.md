## CATEGORY 29: Referral & Affiliate Programs

### 29.01 — Referral Link Generator
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Marketing Mike | **Pain:** You tell clients "refer a friend!" but there's no way to track who referred whom. Rewards are based on the honor system. | **Cost:** Referral programs without tracking have 5x lower participation than tracked programs.
**AIDA:** **A:** "You told 200 clients to refer friends. 30 did. You have no idea which 30." **I:** "Unique referral URLs per client. When a referral converts, the source is automatically credited." **D:** "Sarah shares her link → 3 friends click → 1 signs up → Sarah gets instant notification: 'Your referral John just signed up! You earned $100 credit.' Sarah shares more." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 29.02 — Referral Conversion Tracker
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Marketing Mike | **Pain:** Someone mentions they were referred by a client. You manually credit the referrer — if you remember. | **Cost:** Uncredited referrals = unhappy referrers who stop referring.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your best referrer sent you 5 clients. You credited them for 2 because you forgot the others. They stopped referring." **I:** "Automatic referral source tracking → referrer credited instantly → thank-you notification sent." **D:** "New client signs up via Sarah's link → CRM records 'Referred by Sarah' → Sarah gets email: 'Thanks! John just signed up through your referral. Your reward: $100.' Zero manual tracking." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 29.03 — Referral Reward Payout
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Bookkeeper Beth | **Pain:** Referral credits accumulate but nobody processes the payouts. Referrers wait months for their rewards. Enthusiasm dies. | **Cost:** Delayed rewards kill referral program momentum.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your referrer has $300 in credits. They've been waiting 4 months for a payout. They've stopped referring." **I:** "When credits reach the payout threshold, reward auto-processes: gift card, account credit, or commission payment." **D:** "Credit hits $100 → auto-payout via Stripe → referrer gets: 'Your $100 referral reward has been sent! Keep referring — next reward is just one referral away.'" **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 29.04 — Partner Performance Report
**Personas:** Owner Omar | **Pain:** You have 30 referral partners. You invest time in all of them equally. Some produce 10 referrals, others produce zero. | **Cost:** Equal investment in unequal partners = wasted relationship-building time.
**AIDA:** **A:** "You took 30 partners to lunch this quarter. 5 of them generated all 25 referrals. The other 25 partners: zero." **I:** "Monthly scorecard per partner: referrals sent, conversions, revenue generated, payout earned." **D:** "Report: 'Top partner: Dr. Smith CPA — 8 referrals, 5 converted, $12K revenue, $1,200 paid. Bottom 15 partners: 0 referrals combined.' Invest in Dr. Smith. Re-engage or drop the bottom 15." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 29.05 — Referral Request Campaign
**Personas:** Marketing Mike, Owner Omar | **Pain:** You never systematically ask happy clients for referrals. Referrals happen randomly instead of predictably. | **Cost:** Referral-driven businesses grow 4x faster. Without systematic asking, you're leaving 75% of referral potential untapped.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your happiest clients would gladly refer you — but you never asked. Referrals shouldn't be accidental." **I:** "Quarterly campaign to high-satisfaction clients: personalized referral request with incentive and easy-share link." **D:** "'Hi Sarah, you rated us 5/5 on your last project! Know anyone who could benefit from the same results? Share your referral link and earn $100 per signup.' 15% of happy clients refer. That's 20 new warm leads per quarter." **Action:** "$14/mo."

## CATEGORY 30: Upselling & Cross-Selling

### 30.01 — Usage-Based Upsell Trigger
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Agency Alex | **Pain:** Clients hitting their plan limits are the most natural upsell candidates. But nobody monitors usage to identify them. | **Cost:** Clients hitting limits without upsell either get frustrated or self-serve a workaround. You miss revenue.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your client used 95% of their allocated hours. They're about to either stop working or find a cheaper provider for the overflow." **I:** "Alert at 80% usage: 'ABC Corp at 80% of 20-hour allocation. 12 days remaining. Recommend upgrade discussion.'" **D:** "You call: 'I noticed you're using our services heavily this month — that's great! Want me to bump you to the 30-hour plan so you don't hit the cap?' Natural, timely, appreciated." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 30.02 — Cross-Sell Recommendation Engine
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Marketing Mike | **Pain:** Clients using one service don't know you offer related services. The plumber doesn't know you also do HVAC. | **Cost:** Average service business could increase revenue 25% from cross-selling existing clients.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your CROP client doesn't know you also offer annual report filing. They're paying someone else $200/year for something you could do." **I:** "Based on current services, auto-recommend complementary offerings via email after project completion or at quarterly review." **D:** "CROP client receives: 'Did you know we also handle annual report filing? Most of our CROP clients save $150/year by bundling. Add it here: [link].' 20% add the service." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 30.03 — Annual Review Upsell Package
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Agency Alex | **Pain:** Renewal conversations are about price, not value. You should be expanding, not defending. | **Cost:** Flat renewals mean flat revenue. Without expansion selling, growth requires new clients only.
**AIDA:** **A:** "15 clients renewed last quarter at the same price. Zero upgraded. Your revenue is flat despite a growing client base." **I:** "30 days before renewal: auto-generate an expanded package proposal showing current plan vs. upgraded plan with additional services and value." **D:** "Renewal email: 'Your current plan: $299/mo. Upgraded plan: $499/mo with added SEO monitoring + quarterly reviews + priority support. Lock in before renewal: [link].' 30% upgrade. Revenue per client: +$200/mo." **Action:** "$24/mo."

### 30.04 — Abandoned Cart Recovery
**Personas:** Marketing Mike, Owner Omar | **Pain:** Someone starts your checkout process and doesn't complete it. You have no idea who they are or why they stopped. | **Cost:** Average cart abandonment rate: 70%. Recovering even 10% of abandoned carts is pure found revenue.
**AIDA:** **A:** "70% of people who start your checkout leave without paying. Last month: 30 started, 9 completed, 21 abandoned. That's $21,000 in lost revenue." **I:** "Detect abandoned checkouts → send recovery sequence: 1-hour email, 24-hour email with FAQ, 72-hour email with incentive." **D:** "21 abandoned carts → recovery sequence sent → 4 complete purchase (19% recovery). Revenue recovered: $4,000 from emails that cost nothing." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 30.05 — Service Anniversary Offer
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Marketing Mike | **Pain:** Client's been with you for a year. Nobody acknowledges it. No special offer. Another year of flat revenue. | **Cost:** Anniversary moments are peak loyalty and expansion opportunities — wasted when ignored.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your client's 1-year anniversary was last week. Your competitor sent their clients anniversary gifts. To celebrate, here's an exclusive 15% discount on any additional service. Valid for 30 days.' 25% of anniversary clients add a service." **Action:** "$14/mo."

## CATEGORY 31: Client Retention & Renewals

### 31.01 — Renewal Reminder Sequence
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Manager Maria | **Pain:** Annual contracts renew quietly — or lapse quietly. Without reminders, 15% don't renew simply because nobody brought it up. | **Cost:** 15% preventable churn = 15% of annual revenue lost passively.
**AIDA:** **A:** "5 annual contracts lapsed last year. 3 of them would have renewed if someone had simply reminded them." **I:** "Multi-step reminders: 60, 30, 14, 7, and 1 day before renewal — with renewal link and summary of value delivered." **D:** "60-day: 'Your renewal is coming up. Here's what we accomplished this year.' 30-day: 'Renew now and lock in your current rate.' 7-day: 'Final reminder — your service continues uninterrupted when you renew.' 95% renewal rate." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 31.02 — At-Risk Client Detector
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Manager Maria | **Pain:** Clients don't announce they're about to churn. They go quiet. Login frequency drops. Support tickets increase. Engagement declines. But nobody's monitoring. | **Cost:** Detecting churn risk 30 days earlier gives you a 60% chance of saving the client.
**AIDA:** **A:** "3 clients churned last month. All 3 showed warning signs — decreased login, no email opens, late payments — for 60+ days before canceling." **I:** "AI scores churn risk from: login frequency, email engagement, support ticket sentiment, payment punctuality, and usage trends." **D:** "Alert: 'ABC Corp churn risk: HIGH (score 82/100). Signals: no login in 30 days, last 2 emails unopened, payment was 7 days late.' You call. They had an unresolved issue. You fix it. They stay." **Action:** "$29/mo."

### 31.03 — Win-Back Campaign for Churned Clients
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Marketing Mike | **Pain:** Client cancels. You accept it and move on. 90 days later, their situation changed — they'd come back if you asked. But you never ask. | **Cost:** Winning back a former client costs 1/5 of acquiring a new one. Not asking is leaving money on the table.
**AIDA:** **A:** "8 clients churned last quarter. At least 2 would come back if you reached out. But you haven't contacted them since they left." **I:** "30/60/90-day post-churn campaigns: check in, share what's new, offer a return incentive." **D:** "Day 90 email: 'We've missed you! Since you left, we've added [new feature]. We'd love to welcome you back with 20% off your first 3 months.' 8-12% win-back rate." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 31.04 — Client Health Score Calculator
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Manager Maria, Agency Alex | **Pain:** Some clients are happy. Some are at risk. You can't tell the difference until one cancels. | **Cost:** No health scoring = reactive retention instead of proactive.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Which of your 50 clients are thriving and which are about to leave? You're guessing. Data shouldn't be a guessing game." **I:** "Composite score from: portal login frequency, email engagement, support ticket volume, payment timeliness, NPS responses, and usage." **D:** "Dashboard: Green (healthy): 38 clients. Yellow (watch): 8 clients. Red (at-risk): 4 clients. The 4 red clients get immediate outreach. Churn cut in half." **Action:** "$24/mo."

### 31.05 — Loyalty Milestone Celebrator
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Solo Steve | **Pain:** Your 5-year client gets the same treatment as your 5-month client. Loyalty goes unrecognized. | **Cost:** Unrecognized loyalty makes long-term clients feel taken for granted — they become open to competitors.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your longest client has been with you 5 years. Have you ever acknowledged it? Your competitor would have sent a gift basket at year 1." **I:** "Auto-celebrate milestones: 1 year, 2 years, 5 years — with personalized messages, gifts, or account perks." **D:** "'Congratulations on 5 years with us, Sarah! You're one of our founding clients. As a thank you, we're adding priority support to your account — on us.' Sarah shares it on LinkedIn. Free marketing + unbreakable loyalty." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 31.06 — Service Usage Report for Clients
**Personas:** Agency Alex, Owner Omar | **Pain:** Clients pay monthly but don't see the value because nobody shows them what they're getting. They only see the bill. | **Cost:** Invisible value → perceived overpricing → churn.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your client pays $2,000/month. They see an invoice. They don't see the 45 tasks completed, 12 meetings held, and $18K in leads generated." **I:** "Monthly/quarterly report auto-sent: work delivered, hours invested, results achieved, and ROI calculation." **D:** "Client receives: 'This month: 32 tasks completed, 15 hours of consulting, 8 new leads generated, estimated value: $12,000. Your investment: $2,000. ROI: 500%.' Cancellation? Not happening." **Action:** "$24/mo."

## CATEGORY 32: Client Offboarding & Churn

### 32.01 — Cancellation Reason Collector
**Personas:** Owner Omar, Manager Maria | **Pain:** Client cancels. You don't know why. Was it price? Quality? Timing? Competitor? You can't fix what you don't diagnose. | **Cost:** Repeating the same churn pattern because you never identify the root cause.
**AIDA:** **A:** "12 clients cancelled this year. How many told you why? If the answer is fewer than 12, you're missing critical data." **I:** "Cancel flow includes a required reason survey: price, quality, timing, competitor, no longer need, other." **D:** "Q1 analysis: 5 cancellations — 3 cited price, 1 cited timing, 1 cited competitor. Price is the #1 issue. You introduce a lower tier. Q2 cancellations drop 60%." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 32.02 — Cancellation Save Offer
**Personas:** Owner Omar | **Pain:** Client clicks cancel. It processes immediately. Nobody tried to save them. | **Cost:** 20-30% of cancellations are saveable with the right offer at the right moment.
**AIDA:** **A:** "20% of clients who cancel would have stayed if you'd offered a discount, a pause, or a downgrade. But nobody asked." **I:** "Before cancellation finalizes: 'Before you go — would any of these help? 20% off for 3 months / Pause your account for 60 days / Downgrade to our Basic plan.'" **D:** "Client clicks cancel → save offer appears → 25% choose to pause or downgrade instead of leaving. That's 25% fewer churned clients." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 32.03 — Account Deactivation Processor
**Personas:** Manager Maria, Compliance Carol | **Pain:** Client cancels but their portal access stays active for months. Their data isn't archived. Their hosting keeps running. You're paying for a ghost account. | **Cost:** Orphaned accounts cost money (hosting, licenses) and create security risk.
**AIDA:** **A:** "You have 8 cancelled clients still accessing your portal. Their hosting is still running at $15/month each. That's $120/month in waste." **I:** "Grace period ends → portal access revoked → hosting deprovisioned → data archived → confirmation sent." **D:** "Day 30 post-cancellation: account deactivated, data exported and archived, hosting terminated, confirmation email: 'Your account has been closed. Your data is archived for 90 days per our retention policy.'" **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 32.04 — Final Invoice & Data Export
**Personas:** Bookkeeper Beth, Manager Maria | **Pain:** Client leaves with an outstanding balance. Nobody sends a final invoice. They also want their data but nobody packages it. | **Cost:** Uncollected final invoices. GDPR/CCPA data portability violations.
**AIDA:** **A:** "3 churned clients owe a combined $2,100 in final invoices. None have been sent." **I:** "Offboarding triggers: final invoice generated for any remaining balance + data export prepared and downloadable." **D:** "Offboarding initiated → final invoice of $350 sent → data export prepared: 'Your account data is ready for download. Link valid for 30 days.' Clean, professional, compliant." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 32.05 — Offboarding Feedback Email
**Personas:** Owner Omar | **Pain:** Client left 30 days ago. They've had time to reflect. Their feedback now would be more thoughtful than the day they cancelled — but nobody asks. | **Cost:** Missing post-churn insights that could prevent future churn.
**AIDA:** **A:** "The best feedback comes 30 days after cancellation — when emotions have settled and reflection has happened. You never ask." **I:** "7-30 days post-offboarding: 'We respect your decision. As we continue to improve, your honest feedback would mean a lot. What could we have done differently?'" **D:** "Response: 'Honestly, the service was great but I needed more reporting. If you add that, I'd come back.' You add reporting. You win them back 6 months later." **Action:** "$9/mo."

### 33.04 — Purchase Order Generator

Vendor receives it. Accounting has the record. Clean procurement." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 33.05 — Vendor Payment Scheduler

Late cost $75 in fees." **I:** "Invoice approved → payment auto-scheduled for the optimal date based on terms (Net 30 = day 29)." **D:** "Every vendor paid on time, every time. Cash flow optimized. Zero late fees. Vendor relationships: excellent." **Action:** "$14/mo."

## CATEGORY 34: Inventory, Equipment & Assets

### 34.01 — Supply Level Monitor
**Personas:** Field Service Fred, Manager Maria | **Pain:** You run out of a critical supply mid-job because nobody tracked inventory. Emergency supply runs waste time and cost premium prices. | **Cost:** Emergency supply runs cost 30-50% more than planned ordering.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your tech ran out of copper fittings mid-job. Emergency supply run: 45 minutes + 40% markup. Client waited." **I:** "Track inventory levels. Alert when stock drops below reorder threshold. Auto-generate purchase orders." **D:** "Alert: 'Copper fittings: 12 remaining (reorder point: 20). PO auto-generated for 50 units from preferred vendor.' Order placed before you run out. Zero emergency runs." **Action:** "$19/mo."

### 34.02 — Equipment Maintenance Scheduler
**Personas:** Field Service Fred, Owner Omar | **Pain:** Equipment breaks down because preventive maintenance was forgotten. Repair costs 5x more than maintenance. | **Cost:** Reactive maintenance costs 5-10x more than preventive maintenance.

Auto-reminders before each service is due." **D:** "Alert: 'Truck #3: oil change due in 200 miles. Service van #7: brake inspection due April 20.' Schedule maintenance proactively. Equipment lasts longer. Surprise breakdowns: eliminated." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 34.03 — Asset Depreciation Calculator

### 34.04 — License/Subscription Renewal Tracker
**Personas:** Manager Maria, Owner Omar | **Pain:** SaaS subscriptions auto-renew. Some you don't use anymore. Some have cheaper alternatives. Nobody tracks which tools you're actually paying for. | **Cost:** Average business wastes 25-30% of SaaS spend on unused or underutilized subscriptions.

Alerts before renewal for review-and-renew or cancel decisions." **D:** "Alert: 'Subscription XYZ ($99/mo) renews in 14 days. Last login: 6 months ago. Action: cancel or justify renewal.' Cancel 12 unused tools → save $1,800/month." **Action:** "$14/mo."

## CATEGORY 35: Document Management

### 35.01 — Auto-File Documents by Type
**Personas:** Manager Maria, Compliance Carol | **Pain:** Incoming documents — contracts, invoices, IDs, insurance certs — land in one inbox. Someone manually sorts them into folders. They don't. | **Cost:** Unfiled documents are unfindable. Compliance audits reveal missing paperwork.
**AIDA:** **A:** "An auditor asked for your client insurance certificates. 40% weren't in the right folder. Finding them took 3 hours." **I:** "AI classifies incoming documents (contract, invoice, ID, insurance) and auto-files them to the correct folder with metadata tags." **D:** "Document uploaded → AI: 'This is an insurance certificate for ABC Corp, expiring June 2026.' → Filed in /ABC Corp/Insurance/ → tagged: insurance, 2026, GL. Found in 3 seconds, not 3 hours." **Action:** "$24/mo."

### 35.02 — Document Version Control
**Personas:** Manager Maria, Agency Alex | **Pain:** Proposal_v2_FINAL_FINAL_v3.docx. Sound familiar? Nobody knows which version is current. Changes overwrite without history. | **Cost:** Working from outdated documents creates errors. No rollback when someone makes a mistake.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your team spent 2 hours on the wrong version of the proposal. The client saw outdated pricing. Embarrassing and costly." **I:** "Every document save creates a new version. Latest version is always clearly marked. Full history available for rollback." **D:** "File saved → v4 created → v3 archived. Team always sees: 'Proposal v4 (current) — last edited by Sarah, April 14.' Open v3, v2, or v1 anytime." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 35.03 — Bulk Document Generator (Mail Merge)
**Personas:** Compliance Carol, Owner Omar | **Pain:** 50 clients need personalized certificates, letters, or compliance documents. Creating them one by one takes a full day. | **Cost:** 8 hours of repetitive document creation. Prone to copy-paste errors.
**AIDA:** **A:** "You need to send personalized compliance letters to 50 clients. At 10 minutes each, that's 8 hours of copy-paste." **I:** "Upload a template + contact list → generate 50 personalized documents in seconds." **D:** "Template: 'Dear [Name], Your [Entity] annual report is due [Date]...' + 50-row contact list → 50 personalized letters generated in 30 seconds. Zero errors. 8 hours saved." **Action:** "$24/mo."

### 35.04 — Document Expiry Tracker
**Personas:** Compliance Carol, Manager Maria | **Pain:** Client licenses, insurance certs, and certifications expire. Nobody tracks it until a compliance check reveals the gap. | **Cost:** Expired documents = compliance violations. Service delivery with expired insurance = full liability exposure.
**AIDA:** **A:** "Your client's insurance certificate expired 2 months ago. You've been providing service to an uninsured client. If something goes wrong, you're liable." **I:** "All documents with expiry dates tracked. Alerts at 90, 60, and 30 days before expiry." **D:** "Alert: 'ABC Corp insurance certificate expires in 30 days. Request updated cert now.' You request. They provide. No gap in coverage." **Action:** "$14/mo."

### 35.05 — OCR & Searchable PDF Converter
**Personas:** Manager Maria, Compliance Carol | **Pain:** Scanned documents are just images. You can't search them, copy text, or extract data. Finding info in 500 scanned pages means scrolling through every one. | **Cost:** Unsearchable documents might as well not exist. Hours lost hunting for information.
**AIDA:** **A:** "You need to find a specific clause in 200 pages of scanned contracts. That's 3 hours of scrolling and reading." **I:** "Upload scanned document → OCR extracts text → searchable PDF generated. Search for any word or phrase." **D:** "Search 'indemnification' across 200 pages → found on page 47, 89, and 152. 3 seconds instead of 3 hours." **Action:** "$19/mo."
