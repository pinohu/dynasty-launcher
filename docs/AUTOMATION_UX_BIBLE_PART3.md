## CATEGORY 9: Scheduling & Appointments

### 9.01 — Self-Service Booking Page
**Primary Personas:** Solo Steve, Field Service Fred, Agency Alex
**Pain Point:** Scheduling requires phone tag or email chains. 3-5 exchanges to find a mutual time. Prospects drop off during the friction.
**Cost of Inaction:** 30% of prospects abandon scheduling if it takes more than 2 exchanges. You lose deals not because of quality or price, but because booking was hard.
**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "It took 4 emails over 3 days to schedule a 30-minute call. 30% of your prospects gave up before email #3."
- **Interest:** "A branded booking page shows your real-time availability with service selection. Client picks a time, both parties get confirmation. Zero back-and-forth."
- **Desire:** "Prospect clicks 'Book a Call' → sees available slots → picks Thursday at 2 PM → confirmation email arrives with video link and prep instructions. Total elapsed time: 45 seconds."
- **Action:** "Booking page live in 10 minutes. Embed on your website or share the link. $19/mo."

### 9.02 — Appointment Confirmation Email
**Primary Personas:** All personas
**Pain Point:** People book appointments and forget the details. When, where, what to bring, how to join the video call. They no-show because they couldn't find the info.
**Cost of Inaction:** No-show rates increase 40% without proper confirmations. Clients show up unprepared, wasting the meeting. Professional perception suffers.
**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "Your prospect no-showed because they couldn't find the Zoom link in their email. You had sent it in the booking confirmation — buried in paragraph 4."
- **Interest:** "Branded confirmation email with date, time, timezone, location or video link, prep instructions, and a one-click add-to-calendar button — designed for scannability."
- **Desire:** "Every booking gets a beautiful confirmation: big bold time/date, one-click calendar add, video link as a standalone button, and 'Here's what to prepare.' No-show rate drops 40%."
- **Action:** "Confirmation sends automatically on every booking. Template included. $14/mo."

### 9.03 — Appointment Reminder (24-Hour)
**Primary Personas:** All personas
**Pain Point:** People forget appointments. Even important ones. A 24-hour reminder is the difference between a no-show and a show.
**Cost of Inaction:** Without reminders, no-show rates are 20-30%. Each no-show costs 30-60 minutes of dead time plus the lost opportunity.
**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "You had 5 no-shows this month. That's 5 hours of your life you'll never get back. A $0.05 text message would have prevented 3 of them."
- **Interest:** "24 hours before every appointment: email + SMS reminder with all details and a one-click reschedule option if they can't make it."
- **Desire:** "No-shows drop from 20% to 8%. The 12% who would have ghosted either show up or reschedule — either way, your time isn't wasted."
- **Action:** "Enable on your calendar. Reminders are automatic. $14/mo."

### 9.04 — Appointment Reminder (1-Hour)
**Primary Personas:** All personas
**Pain Point:** The 24-hour reminder got them to remember. The 1-hour reminder gets them to actually stop what they're doing and join.
**Cost of Inaction:** People acknowledge the 24-hour reminder, then get busy and forget again. The final nudge is what gets them on the call.
**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "They confirmed yesterday. Today they're in back-to-back meetings and forgot. A 1-hour SMS would have caught them."
- **Interest:** "Quick SMS 60 minutes before: 'Reminder: Your call with [Company] is in 1 hour at [time]. Join here: [link].' Short, direct, impossible to miss."
- **Desire:** "Phone buzzes at 1 PM: 'Your 2 PM call is in 1 hour. Join here: [link].' They set a personal reminder. They join on time. Meeting happens."
- **Action:** "Add to your reminder stack. $9/mo add-on to 24-hour reminder."
