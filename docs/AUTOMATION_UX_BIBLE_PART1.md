## PART I — CORE BUYER PERSONAS

These 10 personas represent the archetypes who purchase service-business automation. Every one of the 347 automations maps to 1-3 of these personas as primary buyers.

---

### PERSONA 1: "Solo Steve" — The Solo Service Provider

**Demographics:** Age 28-45, male or female, runs a one-person service business (cleaning, handyman, consulting, bookkeeping, photography). Revenue $40K-$150K. Tech comfort: basic (uses phone heavily, minimal desktop).

**Psychographics:** Wears every hat. Proud of being independent but quietly drowning. Does client work during the day, admin at night. Misses follow-ups because there's no system. Spouse/partner is frustrated by the hours. Knows "there has to be a better way" but is terrified of complex software. Has tried one or two tools and abandoned them.

**Goals:** Stop working 70-hour weeks. Never miss a lead again. Look professional and organized to clients. Eventually hire someone but needs systems first.

**Frustrations:** Leads slip through cracks. Invoices go out late. No time to market. Every tool demo assumes you have a team. Can't justify $300/mo for software when revenue fluctuates.

**Buying behavior:** Needs the cheapest entry point. Will pay $19-49/mo for something that visibly saves time THIS WEEK. Buys on mobile. Cancels fast if onboarding takes more than 15 minutes. Word-of-mouth and Facebook group recommendations drive decisions.

**Key quote:** *"I didn't start a business to do paperwork all night."*

**Primary automation categories:** 1-5, 8-9, 12-15, 21, 35

### PERSONA 2: "Manager Maria" — The Small Team Leader

**Demographics:** Age 32-50, manages a service business with 3-15 employees. Revenue $250K-$2M. Roles: operations manager, office manager, or owner who's transitioned from doing the work to managing. Tech comfort: moderate.

**Psychographics:** Constantly context-switching between managing staff, handling client escalations, and trying to build repeatable processes. Knows the business runs on tribal knowledge that lives in people's heads. Has experienced the pain of a key employee leaving and taking all the process knowledge with them. Wants to systematize everything but keeps getting pulled into fires.

**Goals:** Build processes that don't depend on any single person. Reduce client complaints caused by dropped balls. Get visibility into what her team is actually doing. Free herself from being the bottleneck.

**Frustrations:** Team members forget steps. Client onboarding is different every time. No way to know if follow-ups are happening. Reports require manually pulling data from 5 places. Staff turnover means retraining from scratch.

**Buying behavior:** Willing to pay $99-299/mo for proven solutions. Needs to see ROI in the first 30 days. Influenced by case studies from similar-sized businesses. Will champion the purchase internally but needs to justify to the owner.

**Key quote:** *"Every time someone quits, we lose six months of institutional knowledge."*

**Primary automation categories:** 4-5, 8, 10-11, 18, 22-23, 36-37

### PERSONA 3: "Owner Omar" — The Growth-Stage Business Owner

**Demographics:** Age 35-55, owns a service business with 15-50 employees. Revenue $2M-$10M. Has an office manager and department leads but still makes most strategic decisions. Tech comfort: moderate to high (evaluates tools himself).

**Psychographics:** Past the survival stage, now focused on scaling. Realizes that what got him here won't get him there. The business grows linearly with headcount and he wants to break that pattern. Reads business books, listens to podcasts, attends masterminds. Knows automation is the answer but doesn't know where to start because the landscape is overwhelming.

**Goals:** Scale revenue without proportionally scaling headcount. Systematize sales so it doesn't depend on him. Get real-time visibility into business health. Build a business that runs without him being in every decision.

**Frustrations:** Revenue grows but margins stay flat because he keeps hiring. Sales pipeline is a black box. Can't compare marketing channel performance. Spends weekends doing the thinking work he can't get to during the week.

**Buying behavior:** Invests $299-999/mo on infrastructure. Buys annual plans for discount. Wants a strategic partner, not just a tool. Influenced by peer recommendations at industry events and masterminds. Will pay for done-for-you setup.

**Key quote:** *"I've got 40 employees and still feel like a one-man show."*

**Primary automation categories:** 1, 5-7, 10, 15-17, 23, 27, 30-31, 38, 45

**Demographics:** Age 40-60, operates 3-20 locations of a service business (dental offices, cleaning franchises, fitness studios, salons). Revenue $5M-$50M. Has location managers. Tech comfort: high (already using multiple systems).

**Key quote:** *"My best location runs completely differently from my worst, and I can't figure out why."*

### PERSONA 5: "Startup Sam" — The Digital-First Founder

**Demographics:** Age 25-40, launching or running a tech-enabled service business. Revenue $0-$500K. Background in tech or business. Building the business "the right way" from day one. Tech comfort: very high.

**Psychographics:** Wants to automate everything before scaling. Thinks in systems and workflows. Comfortable with APIs and no-code tools. **Frustrations:** Too many tool options, analysis paralysis. Building custom automations takes longer than expected. Hard to find pre-built workflows that match his exact needs. Wants templates he can customize, not build from scratch.

**Buying behavior:** Price-sensitive on recurring costs but will pay one-time for templates and setups. Prefers self-serve. Loves marketplaces (AppSumo, n8n template library). Buys lifetime deals. Will pay $49-149 for a workflow template that saves him 10 hours of building.

**Key quote:** *"If I'm doing it more than twice, it should be automated."*

## PART II — JOURNEY MAPS

### Universal Buyer Journey (All Personas)

**Stage 1: UNAWARE**
The prospect doesn't know automation exists for their specific pain. They're doing everything manually and think that's just how it is. They Google things like "how to organize client files" not "client document automation."

**Stage 2: PROBLEM-AWARE**
Something breaks — a missed follow-up costs them a $5,000 client. An invoice goes out 3 weeks late. They realize the manual way is actively costing them money. They start searching for solutions but don't know the right terms.

**Stage 3: SOLUTION-AWARE**
They discover that automation exists for their problem. They've seen an n8n demo, heard about Zapier, or a peer mentioned "we automated our onboarding." They start evaluating but are overwhelmed by options. They search for comparisons, templates, "best tool for X."

**Stage 4: PRODUCT-AWARE**
They've found your specific automation offering. They're comparing it to alternatives and DIY. Key questions: Does it work for MY industry? How long to set up? What if it breaks? What does it cost vs. what I'm spending now?

**Stage 5: MOST AWARE**
They know your product, understand the value, and are deciding between action and inaction. They need a final push — a trial, a demo, a guarantee, or a peer testimonial. Price objection is really a risk objection.

## PART IV — PAIN POINTS & AIDA MESSAGING PER AUTOMATION

Format for each automation:
- **Pain Point**: The specific problem this automation solves (what keeps the persona up at night)
- **Cost of Inaction**: What happens if they DON'T automate this
- **AIDA**: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action messaging framework

## CATEGORY 1: Lead Generation & Prospecting

### 1.01 — Google Business Profile Monitor

**Primary Personas:** Solo Steve, Marketing Mike, Owner Omar
**Pain Point:** Business owners don't know when they get a new review, when a competitor outranks them, or when someone asks a question on their GBP — and unanswered reviews and Q&A kill trust.
**Cost of Inaction:** Negative reviews sit unanswered for weeks, damaging reputation. Q&A goes unanswered, losing leads to competitors who respond. Ranking drops go unnoticed until revenue dips.

**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "A 1-star review just sat on your Google listing for 11 days. You didn't know."
- **Interest:** "This automation checks your Google Business Profile every 24 hours for new reviews, Q&A, and ranking changes — and alerts you instantly so nothing slips."
- **Desire:** "Imagine waking up to a Slack message: 'New 5-star review from Sarah J. — here's a suggested thank-you response.' Or getting a heads-up that your ranking dropped from #2 to #5 before your competitors notice you're slipping."
- **Action:** "Start monitoring your GBP in 60 seconds — connect your listing, choose your alert channel, done. $19/mo. First 14 days free."

---

### 1.02 — Competitor New Review Alert

**Primary Personas:** Marketing Mike, Owner Omar
**Pain Point:** Competitors get reviews (positive or negative) and you have no idea. You're flying blind on competitive intelligence.
**Cost of Inaction:** Competitors improve their reputation unchallenged. You miss opportunities to learn from their negative reviews (their weakness = your marketing angle). You don't know when a competitor is gaining momentum.

**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "Your biggest competitor just got 12 five-star reviews this month. Did you know?"
- **Interest:** "Get a notification every time a competitor receives a review — including what was said, their star rating, and whether they responded."
- **Desire:** "When their client leaves a 2-star review complaining about slow response times, you'll know within hours. That's your opening to run an ad: 'Same-day service, guaranteed.'"
- **Action:** "Add up to 5 competitor listings. Alerts start in under 2 minutes. $29/mo."

---

### 1.03 — New Business Filing Alert

**Primary Personas:** Solo Steve (B2B services), Owner Omar, Compliance Carol
**Pain Point:** New businesses register every day in your area and they all need services (accounting, insurance, web design, CROP services) — but by the time you find out, competitors have already reached them.
**Cost of Inaction:** First-mover advantage goes to whoever contacts new businesses first. Every week you don't monitor filings is a week of leads going to competitors with better systems.

**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "47 new businesses were filed in your county last month. Your competitors contacted them first."
- **Interest:** "This automation monitors your state's Secretary of State database daily and delivers a clean CSV of every new business: name, address, filing date, entity type."
- **Desire:** "Picture this: every morning at 8am, a fresh list of businesses that registered yesterday lands in your inbox. You call them before lunch. They haven't even picked a bookkeeper yet."
- **Action:** "Set your state and county filters. First alert arrives tomorrow morning. $29/mo."

### 1.04 — Expired License/Permit Prospector

**Primary Personas:** Compliance Carol, Owner Omar
**Pain Point:** Businesses with expiring licenses desperately need compliance help but don't know it until they get fined. You could be their hero if you reached them first.
**Cost of Inaction:** These businesses will either get fined and then scramble (paying a competitor who happened to find them), or lapse and face penalties. You miss a warm audience that has a documented, urgent need.

**AIDA:**
- **Attention:** "312 businesses in your area have licenses expiring in the next 90 days. None of them know you exist."
